About Guelph


The City of Guelph is a large community focused on integrity, excellence, and wellness. A municipality with many departments, Guelph’s City Hall is located on 1 Carden Street, which accommodates Accessibility – my department – Planning, Transportation, and many more.

With Guelph’s main goal being supporting the people, their slogan is “Making a Difference” and that’s what they strive to do in every department. With the AODA web content compliance date coming up in 2021, Guelph is one of the municipalities that is ahead of the game.

Fun Fact: The wire coat hanger was invented here in the 1920s!

My Job

As an Accessibility Assistant for City of Guelph’s Accessible Web Content project it was my job to assist the only other person on the project (my supervisor). With a website of over 4000 pages, and over 6000 documents this is a very large project for only two people! When I had started at the City of Guelph the project had just began and it didn’t really have a solid plan or direction. With my supervisor, Leanne, we sat down and gave the project direction, goals, and a solid plan. I had several tasks that reached across the board, some these include:

Tracking and Managing the Project

  • Creating a project schedule and breaking it up into small iterations
  • Translating the schedule into a Gantt chart with timelines and resources
  • Breaking the project down further and creating an interdepartmental Gantt chart, easily understood by people in the department
  • Creating a master excel spreadsheet to show which departments we had been in contact with

Developing and Delivering Training

  • Developing training for and managing Quality Assurance software, Siteimprove
  • Developing and delivering training for accessible PDFs
  • Developing additional training for the following co-op student to ensure the transition was as smooth as possible

Being a “Help Desk” for Accessibility

  • Assisting employees with the creation of their accessible content by answering questions, sharing techniques, and researching formatting issues
  • Acting as a checkpoint before content was released by ensuring it was accessible
  • Explaing concepts such as accessibility standards, word formatting, and heading structures to employees
  • Creating documents with Frequent Problems and Frequently Asked Question with easy to follow solutions for staff to learn from

My Semester Goals

  • Tech Literacy

    Throughout the past 4 months I have been helping people by explaining technical concepts such as accessible web concepts, difficult pdf functions and tools, and formatting word documents to be accessible. One of my goals was to be able to communicate these concepts in a comprehensive way without sounding condescending. I was able to confidently explain these concepts to people and received positive feedback on my ability to explain them. I learned that it is very important to be thorough, understanding of someone's skill level, and patient when helping people.

  • Managing Large Projects

    In this position I was able to learn many things about managing large projects. I was given the task to create a Gantt chart template for the project as a whole and broke it up further by making Gantt charts for individual departments in order to keep track of the little details as well. During this time we worked with several departments simultaneously and tracking and managing them was very important. I was able to really understand how much work, time, and organization goes into managing projects.

  • Global Understanding

    One of my goals was to be able to view technology with the barriers that some people may face. My 4 months in the Accessibility Assistant position has given me the ability to look at technology and see these barriers on a daily basis, and my part in making it accessible. I was afforded the opportunity to attend Guelph Accessibility Conference where I heard from many speakers and learned about AI technology and its part in Accessibility. I was also able to learn all about Accessible Web design and how a little extra effort can go a long way. I can now confidently say my perspective in my everyday life has changed and I encourage my friends to do their part by making any programs they produce accessible. I was able to make my co-op report website accessible because of the things I learned.

  • Broad Understanding of Accessibility

    While in this position I wanted to have a deep understanding of accessibility issues, web accessibility, and my part in it all. I was able to learn all about accessibility in Microsoft Word, Adobe Acrobat Pro, websites, and using Quality Assurance tools like Siteimprove. If anyone had issues with any of their documents, they would come to me for help. I was tasked with creating training courses for staff, where I learned about the software’s more in depth, discovered issues and searched for solutions, and delivered training to staff. When delivering the training people came up with issues and questions that I didn't often come across but was able to solve within a few minutes due to my understanding of the software and accessibility.
