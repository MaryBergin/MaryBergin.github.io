About the School of Computer Science


The School of Computer Science at the University of Guelph was established in 1971. The School of Computer Science offers two majors, Software Engineering and Computer Science. While not known for its Computing Degree, the University of Guelph and the School of Computer Science is up and coming and has taught many successful individuals the core knowledge required for a career in Computing.

Fun Fact: Guelph was named one of Canadas "Top 20 Tech Towns" in 2019!

My Job

As the School of Computer Science (SoCS) Recruitment and Liaison Support Co-op it was my job to assist with recruiting efforts in attracting high quality students to the School of Computer Science for Fall 2020. Especially in the times of COVID-19, student retention was a large focus of my job, and ensuring students felt comfortable starting their program at the University of Guelph during a pandemic. This job consisted of many things directed towards this effort, some of these include:

Managing the School of Computer Science Social Media Platforms

  • Researching ways to better manage SoCS multiple social media pages (social media integration tools)
  • Responding to current and potential students’ questions/concerns and directing them to resources
  • Creating a comprehensive social media advertisement proposal

Managing the School of Computer Science Website

  • Reorganizing current content and creating new content
  • Managing the flow of SoCS website to ensure information is easy for students to find
  • Creating news stories to share with current and potential students

Researching Recruitment Efforts

  • Researching and compiling different demographics to be targeted
  • Researching and documenting specific demographic targeting plans
  • Researching and presenting social media advertising efforts to focus on

My Semester Goals

  • Time Management

    While working from home I really wanted to be able to manage my time effectively. Throughout the past 4 months I was able to plan out my individual days and weeks according to the work that I had to do and deadlines coming up. Every Monday I took a look at my calendar and upcoming due dates to plan the week. I was then able to individually plan each day and work towards the deadlines I set for myself. This self-organization was really important as I often struggle with time management. Planning the weeks and days allowed me to have specific things to work towards. I found that I was successful in managing my time effectively.

  • Documentation

    A lot of the work I did in this role was focused on writing content and reports on my research. In the past four months I was able to produce useful and well written documents with the help of my supervisor. After producing something I was able to get feedback and make the needed changes to improve my writing. I also produced a document for the co-op student that would come after me to introduce them to the role and things they should be doing. I was successful in achieving this goal, but it was identified that I need to work on my proof reading to ensure that everything is spelt correctly.

  • Drupal 7

    During this co-op term I was able to attend a training session on Drupal 7 and learn a lot about the language. I was tasked with creating content for the School of Computer Science Website as well as making sure that the flow of the site and its components made sense. In the beginning I ran into a few issues with more complicated page layouts, but I was able to reach out for help and achieve the layout I wanted. During the second half of my co-op work term, I was much more confident in Drupal 7 and I didn’t have to ask for help when creating, editing, or removing content from the website. I successfully completed my goal and learned a lot about Drupal 7.

This experience was very beneficial and a great opportunity I will never forget!
